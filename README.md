### Visual Authentication Guide

Authentic White Borneo has recognizable physical characteristics.

**Color:** Light green-white with subtle cream undertones. Not pure white (over-processed, bleached risk) and not yellow-green (oxidized or improperly dried stock).

**Texture:** Very fine, consistent powder. Lighter and more airy than green or red vein powders. Slight static cling when handled is normal and indicates proper dryness.

**Aroma:** Fresh, slightly grassy with light earthy notes. Mild compared to red vein strains. Strong chemical or musty odor signals processing problems or contamination.

**Mouthfeel when tossed and washed:** Fine, disperses quickly. Gritty texture suggests coarser grind — not necessarily quality-indicating but worth noting.

## White Borneo Storage: Preserving Alkaloid Integrity

White vein kratom alkaloids are more sensitive to degradation than red vein products.

The early-harvest mitragynine profile that creates White Borneo's energy character is also more susceptible to UV exposure and oxidation.

### Storage Best Practices

| Factor | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Light exposure | Opaque airtight container | UV degrades mitragynine rapidly |
| Temperature | Cool, consistent — below 75°F | Heat accelerates oxidation |
| Moisture | Desiccant pack in container | Clumping indicates moisture damage |
| Air exposure | Minimize opening frequency | Oxygen exposure degrades alkaloids |
| Location | Dark pantry or cabinet | Avoid bathroom — humidity fluctuates |
| Open container shelf life | Use within 1–2 months | Alkaloid potency declines after opening |
